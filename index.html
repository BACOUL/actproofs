Why ActProofs exists

Automated systems execute actions faster than ever. Responsibility, however, remains with humans and organizations. ActProofs exists to make authorization explicit, provable, and auditable.

Automation executes

AI agents and workflows perform actions continuously and at scale.

Responsibility remains

Someone must be accountable for approving those actions.

Proof is missing

Logs, permissions, and tickets do not constitute explicit authorization evidence.

What is ActProofs?

ActProofs is an open standard that produces a portable, cryptographically verifiable receipt proving that a specific automated action was explicitly authorized by an identified actor, in a defined context, at a given time.

The receipt is file-based, durable, and designed to be verified independently, without trusting a platform.

ActProofs does not execute actions. It does not enforce policies. It does not manage identities or access control.

It provides evidence of authorization — nothing more, nothing less.

Legitimate use cases

AI & autonomous agents

Prove that a human or organization explicitly authorized an AI-driven action, such as publishing content, modifying data, or contacting users.

Automated workflows

Maintain a clean separation between approval and execution in CI/CD, data pipelines, or operational automation.

Audit & compliance

Produce clear, portable authorization evidence for audits, investigations, and insurance claims.

Create & verify

ActProofs can be created locally and verified publicly. No account is required to verify an authorization receipt.

Create

Declare the intended action and authorization context locally. Receive a signed ActProof that can be archived or shared. 

POST /v1/authorize
{
  "actor": "org:example",
  "action": "publish-content",
  "context": "blog.example.com",
  "timestamp": "2026-01-03T20:45:00Z"
}

Verify

Verify the cryptographic integrity and declared context of an ActProof independently, online or offline. 

GET /verify?proof=actproof.json
→ valid

Documentation

The ActProofs specification defines the proof format, signing model, and verification rules.

Proof format

File-based JSON structure describing authorization, context, and signature.

Signing model

Deterministic, auditable signing without implicit trust.

Verification

Stateless verification rules usable by any independent party.

Security & trust

Cryptographic integrity

ActProofs relies on standard cryptographic primitives. Any tampering invalidates the proof.

No implicit trust

Verification does not rely on trusting a platform or issuer. Proofs are self-contained and portable.

Governance & compliance

Accountability

Clear attribution of authorization decisions.

Audit-ready

Portable artifacts suitable for audits and investigations.

Regulatory alignment

Designed to support modern governance expectations.

Release: ActProofs Website

This mirrors the Proof of Release concept used in related standards, adapted here for authorization evidence.

Relation to TimeProofs

TimeProofs proves when something existed. ActProofs proves that something was authorized.

The two standards are complementary and address different layers of trust in automated systems.
