Automation created a responsibility gap

AI systems already delete data, send messages, trigger workflows, and affect users and businesses.

Logs and permissions explain systems internally. They do not provide independent proof.

Without proof of authorization, responsibility is assumed.

What ActProofs is

ActProofs is a standard and API that produces a portable, cryptographically verifiable receipt proving that a specific automated action was explicitly authorized by a known actor, in a defined context, at a specific time.

Actor → Authorization → ActProof → Automated Action

ActProofs does not execute actions, manage identity, or judge decisions. It only records authorization.

Why now

Automation is scaling faster than accountability. Regulation is catching up.

Automation without provable authorization is becoming unacceptable.
