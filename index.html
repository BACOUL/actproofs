Authorization proof · Automated actions · AI governance

Evidence that an automated action was explicitly authorized.

ActProofs provides a cryptographic, portable receipt proving that a specific automated action — including AI-driven actions — was authorized by a known actor or organization, in a defined context, at a precise point in time.

Why ActProofs exists

Automation is accountable

Automated systems and AI agents trigger actions with legal, financial, or operational impact. Someone must be responsible for authorizing them.

Logs are not evidence

Internal logs are mutable, non-portable, and rarely opposable. They do not constitute independent proof of authorization.

Regulation requires proof

AI Act, NIS2, audits, and insurance processes increasingly require demonstrable authorization of automated decisions.

What ActProofs is (and is not)

What it is

  • Proof of authorization
  • Cryptographic receipt
  • Portable and verifiable file

What it guarantees

  • An action was authorized
  • By a declared actor or entity
  • At a specific time, in context

What it is not

  • No execution engine
  • No identity management
  • No access control system

Mental model

Actor / Organization explicitly authorizes an automated action.

ActProof is generated and attached to the action as evidence.

ActProofs records authorization only. Execution remains out of scope.
