The problem

Implicit decisions

An AI deletes data. A workflow transfers money. Who explicitly authorized it?

No proof of intent

Logs show execution, not approval. Screenshots and policies are not evidence.

Audit dead-end

After an incident, authorization cannot be independently verified.

How ActProofs works

1. Declare

Describe an intended action locally: who, what, why, and scope.

2. Approve

Receive a signed authorization proof from the stateless API.

3. Verify

Verify the proof publicly or offline at any time.

Legitimate use cases

AI & Agents

Human approval before autonomous or irreversible actions.

Automated workflows

Authorization trail without logs or continuous monitoring.

Regulated systems

Clear separation between decision, authorization, and execution.

What ActProofs is not

  • ❌ Not a policy engine
  • ❌ Not an access control system
  • ❌ Not a workflow tool
  • ❌ Not an AI controller
  • ❌ Not a monitoring platform
  • ❌ Not an execution layer

ActProofs proves authorization. Nothing more.

Why an open standard

Neutral, stateless, and vendor-agnostic by design.

Long-term verifiable evidence, independent from any platform.

Relation to TimeProofs

TimeProofs proves when something existed.
ActProofs proves that something was authorized.
