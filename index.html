Authorization standard · Draft

Automated systems act.
Authorization must be provable.

ActProofs defines a neutral standard and API for producing cryptographic proof that an automated or AI-driven action was explicitly authorized — in a given context, at a given time.

The responsibility gap

Automation executes actions with real-world impact. Existing systems record behavior internally, but do not produce independent, portable, verifiable proof of authorization.

When an action is disputed, internal logs are insufficient.

Scope

What ActProofs does

  • Produces cryptographic authorization receipts
  • Links an action to an explicit authorization
  • Enables independent verification and audit

What ActProofs does not do

  • Does not execute, block, or validate actions
  • Does not manage identity or access control
  • Does not judge legality or intent
ActProofs is intentionally narrow by design.

Verification

ActProofs receipts are portable and verifiable without trusting the issuing system. Verification rules are public and can be implemented independently.

Designed for governance and regulation

ActProofs addresses technical requirements emerging from AI governance, auditability, and accountability frameworks, without replacing legal or organizational responsibility.
