Authorization evidence for automated actions

ActProofs is a standard for proving that an automated action was explicitly authorized by an identified actor, in a defined context, at a given time.

It produces a portable, cryptographically verifiable authorization receipt intended for audit, governance, and responsibility.

Scope

An ActProof attests that authorization was explicitly granted before an automated action occurred.

It establishes responsibility for the decision to authorize, not for the technical execution itself.

Out of scope

ActProofs does not:

  • execute actions
  • enforce policies
  • manage identities (IAM)
  • control access rights
  • monitor systems or users

Authorization model

Identified Actor
        ↓
Explicit Authorization
        ↓
ActProof (authorization receipt)
        ↓
Automated Action
(out of scope)

The authorization receipt

An ActProof is:

  • file-based
  • portable
  • cryptographically signed
  • verifiable independently
  • stateless (no server-side storage required)

Legitimate use cases

AI systems and agents
Human or organizational approval before autonomous actions.

Automated workflows
Explicit authorization for data deletion, notifications, or system changes.

Audit, insurance, compliance
Portable authorization evidence for investigations and liability assessment.

Relation to TimeProofs

TimeProofs establishes when something existed.
ActProofs establishes that something was authorized.

The two standards address different layers of trust and are complementary.

Governance

ActProofs is designed for environments where authorization decisions must be explicit, attributable, and reviewable.

The specification is open, neutral, and intended for independent verification.

Open standard · Privacy-first · Stateless · No execution · No identity management
