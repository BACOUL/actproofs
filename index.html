Authorization must be provable.

ActProofs is an open standard defining a cryptographic, portable, and independently verifiable proof that an automated or AI-driven action was explicitly authorized.

Logs are not proof.

What ActProofs provides

ActProofs provides a neutral proof layer that sits between identity systems and execution engines. It records authorization decisions as cryptographic receipts that can be archived, audited, and independently verified.

ActProofs does not execute actions and does not manage identity.

The standard is the product

ActProofs is developed as an open specification designed for interoperability, auditability, and long-term verifiability.
