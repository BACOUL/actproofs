What ActProofs is (and is not)

What you get

A portable authorization receipt proving explicit approval.

Cryptographic signatures, independently verifiable.

What it guarantees

That authorization existed at a given time.

That responsibility can be attributed.

What it’s not

Not execution, not enforcement, not IAM.

Not logging, not monitoring.

Why this exists

Automation risk

Automated systems act faster than traditional controls.

Logs aren’t proof

Execution logs do not prove intent or approval.

Accountability gap

After incidents, authorization is often impossible to demonstrate.
