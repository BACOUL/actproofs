Mission

Make authorization explicit

ActProofs exists to make responsibility explicit in automated systems. When actions happen without human intervention, authorization must be provable ‚Äî not assumed.

Responsibility, not control

ActProofs does not enforce behavior or execute actions. It provides cryptographic evidence that an organization explicitly authorized an action.

How It Works

1. Declare

An organization explicitly declares that a specific action is authorized.

2. Sign

A cryptographically signed ActProof is generated, containing context and timestamp.

3. Verify

Anyone can verify the authorization later, without contacting the issuer.

What ActProofs is

A neutral protocol to record and verify authorization decisions. It proves responsibility without enforcing outcomes.

What ActProofs is not

Not a policy engine. Not an access-control system. Not an AI decision-maker. Not an execution layer.

Why ActProofs

The missing proof

Logs show what happened. Permissions show what could happen. ActProofs proves what was explicitly allowed to happen.

Clear accountability

When systems act autonomously, responsibility must be provable across time and systems.
