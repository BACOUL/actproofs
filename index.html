Evidence of Authorized Actions

ActProofs provides cryptographic, portable, and independently verifiable proof that an automated or AI-driven action was explicitly authorized — without executing, enforcing, or controlling it.

Authorization is recorded as evidence, not as control logic.

Read the ActProofs Standard Regulatory context

The problem

Implicit authorization

Automated systems act, but explicit authorization is rarely provable.

Logs are not proof

Execution traces cannot demonstrate intent, approval, or responsibility.

Audit dead-ends

After incidents, authorization decisions cannot be independently verified.

How it works

1. Declare

Describe the intended automated action and its context.

2. Authorize

Issue a cryptographic authorization proof.

3. Verify

Verify the proof anytime, independently, online or offline.

Legitimate use cases

AI & autonomous agents

Human authorization before autonomous actions.

Automated workflows

Approval evidence without monitoring or enforcement.

Regulated environments

Clear separation between authorization and execution.

What ActProofs is not

❌ Execution engine
❌ Workflow orchestration
❌ AI control layer

❌ Identity management
❌ Access control
❌ Monitoring system

ActProofs proves authorization — nothing more.

Relation to TimeProofs

TimeProofs proves when something existed.
ActProofs proves that something was authorized.

The two standards are complementary and address different questions: existence versus authorization.
