Automated systems act.
Authorization must be provable.

ActProofs defines a neutral standard and API to produce cryptographic proof that an automated or AI-driven action was explicitly authorized, in a given context, at a given time.

The responsibility gap

Automation already triggers actions with real-world impact. Existing systems record events internally, but do not provide independent, portable, verifiable proof of authorization.

When an action is disputed, internal systems are insufficient.

Scope

What ActProofs does

  • Produces cryptographic authorization receipts
  • Links an action to an explicit authorization
  • Supports audit and accountability workflows

What ActProofs does not do

  • Does not execute, block, or validate actions
  • Does not manage identity or access control
  • Does not judge legality, intent, or outcomes
ActProofs is intentionally narrow by design.

Verification

ActProofs receipts are portable and verifiable without trusting the issuing system. Verification rules are public and can be implemented independently.

Designed for governance and regulation

ActProofs addresses emerging requirements around accountability, traceability, and responsibility for automated and AI-driven actions, without replacing legal or organizational responsibility.
