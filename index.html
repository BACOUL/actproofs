ActProofs is an open standard for cryptographic, portable proof of authorization for automated and AI-driven actions.

Stateless

We do not store your data. The proof travels with the data.

Portable

A standalone JSON file verifiable anywhere, anytime.

Independent

Verification relies on cryptography, not on our API uptime.

Blind Notary Architecture

ActProofs operates as a blind notary. We certify when and by whom an authorization was signed, without ever seeing the content of the action.

"ActProofs is a cryptographic receipt proving that an automated action was explicitly authorized by a responsible entity, at a specific time, in a defined context."

Client-Side Hashing

Your infrastructure hashes the authorization manifest (SHA-256) locally. Only this hash is sent to ActProofs for signing. Your PII, prompts, and business logic never leave your perimeter.

Cryptographic Validity

Proofs are signed using Ed25519 keys. The validity of the proof is mathematical and does not depend on the continued existence of the ActProofs service.

Regulatory Alignment

ActProofs provides a technical layer of evidence to support compliance with emerging AI regulations.

EU AI Act (Art. 14)
Provides technical evidence of Human Oversight. Proves a human specifically authorized a high-risk system action.
GDPR Accountability
Demonstrates authorization context for sensitive data processing (e.g., deletion, export) in audit logs.
ISO/IEC 42001
Supports AI Risk Management controls by establishing a verifiable chain of custody for automated decisions.

Key Governance

Root keys are generated in hardware security modules (HSM) and rotated annually. Revocation lists (CRLs) are published daily to ensuring compromised keys cannot issue valid proofs.

Long-term Trust

Because ActProofs uses standard cryptography (Ed25519 + SHA-256), proofs remain verifiable even if the ActProofs entity ceases operations, provided you have retained the public keys.
