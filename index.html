Automation created a responsibility gap

Modern systems execute actions autonomously. Logs and permissions explain behavior internally, but they do not provide independent proof of authorization.

What ActProofs does — and does not do

What ActProofs does

  • Issues cryptographic authorization proofs
  • Produces portable, verifiable receipts
  • Supports audit and accountability workflows

What ActProofs does not do

  • Does not execute or block actions
  • Does not manage identity or access control
  • Does not judge or validate decisions
ActProofs focuses intentionally on authorization proof. Its scope is narrow by design.

Verification and audit

ActProofs produces proofs that can be verified independently, without trusting a single platform. Verification rules and formats are public and reproducible.

Auditors and third parties can validate authorization without accessing the underlying data or systems.

Designed for regulation

ActProofs aligns with regulatory expectations around accountability, traceability, and responsibility for automated and AI-driven actions.

It provides technical proof mechanisms, without substituting legal or compliance processes.
