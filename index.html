Mission

What ACTPROOFS provides

ACTPROOFS is an open standard for producing cryptographic proof that an organization explicitly authorized an action to occur.

Why it matters

Logs show what happened. Permissions show what could happen. ACTPROOFS proves what was allowed to happen.

Responsibility by design

Authorization is declared, signed, and verifiable ‚Äî without enforcing behavior.

Neutral and auditable

ACTPROOFS does not execute actions and does not judge outcomes. It records responsibility.

How It Works

Model: Declare ‚Üí Authorize ‚Üí Execute ‚Üí Verify

1) Declare the action

An organization declares an action that may be performed by an automated system.

2) Issue authorization

A signed authorization proof is generated and attached to the action context.

3) Action executes

The system executes independently of ACTPROOFS.

4) Verify later

Anyone can verify that the action was authorized ‚Äî without contacting the issuer.
