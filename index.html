Authorization · Evidence · Responsibility

Authorization evidence for automated actions

ActProofs is a standard for proving that an automated action was explicitly authorized by an identified actor, in a defined context, at a given time.

Scope

An ActProof attests that authorization was explicitly granted before an automated action occurred.

What it proves

That a specific action was authorized by an identified actor, at a specific time, in a specific context.

What it establishes

Responsibility for the decision to authorize — not for execution.

Out of scope

Execution

No action triggering or enforcement.

Identity

No IAM, no access control.

Monitoring

No tracking or behavioral logs.

Authorization model

Identified Actor
↓
Explicit Authorization
↓
ActProof (authorization receipt)
↓
Automated Action (out of scope)

Legitimate use cases

AI systems & agents

Human approval before autonomous actions.

Automated workflows

Explicit authorization for sensitive operations.

Audit & compliance

Portable authorization evidence for investigations and liability.

Relation to TimeProofs

TimeProofs proves when something existed.
ActProofs proves that something was authorized.

Two complementary standards, addressing different layers of trust.
